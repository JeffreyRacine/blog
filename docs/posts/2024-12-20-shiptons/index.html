Are Guerrilla Business Tactics the New Norm?

Some background may be in order before proceeding further:

  • Our home has two furnaces and one A/C unit

  • They were installed in 2005 when we bought the home

  • We purchased well built energy efficient units designed to last

  • We prefer to have our equipment inspected and serviced regularly before problems arise, rather than waiting for something to go wrong then address the underlying issue

  • We also prefer to maintain equipment that is serviceable for as long as possible rather than dumping it in a landfill prematurely

So, when the units were installed, we took out a maintenance service contract with Shipton’s Heating and Cooling that promised savings by bundling the three units, and we pay $340/year for two cleaning and maintenance visits (one for the furnaces in the Winter, one for the A/C in the Summer), and we provide some parts (replacement furnace humidifier pads and charcoal filters). The units have been maintained and inspected regularly and have performed admirably over the years.

We selected Shipton’s Heating and Cooling as we were told they had a good reputation (at least they did two decades ago). We believe that good equipment, well maintained, can last far beyond the average life expectancy often reported by the industry, which has been and continues to be our experience.

Unfortunately, over the past two years we have come to realize that this particular service contract company employs what might be described as “guerrilla business tactics”, and I penned this post to link to in my online reviews of the company for those interested in the details.

To summarize, it took three strikes to push us over the edge and to terminate our contract with Shipton’s:

  1. Unnecessary Repairs Recommended: Replace your collector box immediately at the cost of $471.88

  2. Hijacking/Ransom: We disabled your A/C unit and are dispatching a sales rep to arrange for a new unit (in the peak of summer, without consultation, on a system performing admirably)

  3. Blackmail: Authorize repairs or we will arrange to have your gas cut off in 42 days (in the dead of winter)

Strike One (Unnecessary Repairs)

In October 2023, during the routine maintenance of our furnaces, the technician who was dispatched (Peter) told us that one unit was showing its age (there was some rust visible on the inside floor of the unit but no evidence of any active leaking), and in the written summary of his visit he wrote “immediate repair, replace collector box” with a price of $417.60+HST.

I emailed Shipton’s dispatch to schedule the repair and received this reply:

I can confirm the price of the Collector Box is $417.60+H.S.T. (which includes the installation). [] As discussed, upon arrival of the part, I’ll be directly in touch with Mrs. Racine & yourself to coordinate a date/time for the repair.

Here is the email I sent to Shipton’s after Pete showed up at the house to do the repair:

This morning the tech arrived to install the collector box we were advised to fix (the same tech who serviced the unit recently and wrote on the attached summary “immediate repair” Collector Box $417+HST). When he arrived he said “I don’t know why I am here, replacing the collector box won’t fix the leak in the unit”… I then said another tech had come and inspected the unit to get the part number, and he said “no one talked to me, I noted the part number.”

I asked him why he wrote “immediate repair collector box” and his reply was “they tell us to write something”. In other words, he is claiming that the collector box did not have a leak yet he wrote that this should be repaired immediately.

This is unacceptable. [] Shipton’s has dispatched two techs to deal with an issue clearly indicated as “Immediate repair” wasting money on two trips not to mention our time and frustration. The purpose of this email is to request that, going forward, kindly put a note on our file to not dispatch this technician to our residence (Peter).

Thanks for your understanding.

So, chalk this visit up to “unnecessary repairs recommended”…

P.S., the furnace continues to perform flawlessly and does not leak.

Strike Two (Ransom)

In June 2024 a technician was dispatched for the annual maintenance of the A/C unit (Stephen).

At the end of the service call, and with zero prior discussion, Steven said he had disabled the A/C unit, that it could no longer be turned on from the inside thermostat, and that he would arrange for the owner (Scott) to come to our house that afternoon to discuss replacement options (it was the middle of summer and the A/C was running perfectly when he arrived). The A/C unit had been operating admirably (and, it still does for the record). I was stunned, and told him the unit works fine. He said it is low on refrigerant and that it could freeze up and leak. I said that years ago we had Shipton’s install two automatic shut off devices to prevent leak damage (one in the drip pan, one in the condensate pump enclosure), which he admitted he was aware of. I instructed him to go outside and re-enable the A/C unit immediately, which he did.

We did not receive a call from Scott to schedule a visit, but the next day were told someone else (Daniel) came to the house unannounced and we were not home, so he left. Daniel’s visit was rescheduled for the following day, and on his second visit he told us that the unit was low on refrigerant and could not be recharged so our only option was to replace it ASAP. I told Daniel how frustrated I was with the Shipton’s technician who disabled our unit without any prior discussion, and how frustrated I was with Shipton’s unannounced visits with no prior notification, but asked for a quote out of curiosity and weeks later received the following email:

“Hey Jeffrey, I spoke with Scott and we figured out more or less what needs to be done. The cost to replace the 3 ton air conditioner and the air handler unit is $15,395 plus HST”.

On July 3 2024 I replied to Daniel’s quote, writing:

“Would you kindly send the details for the quote you kindly provided? We were expecting more than a simple number, in particular the make/model of the A/C and air handler to be installed, cost of all other parts needed for the installation, and a breakdown of the $15,395 total into parts and labor, etc.”

Crickets… zero response to date.

So, chalk this visit up to “we hijacked your A/C unit by disabling it and are seeking ransom in the amount of the cost of replacement”…

P.S., the A/C unit continues to perform flawlessly, and a competitor to Shipton’s gave us the option to recharge the unit immediately or wait until the unit stops working and recharge it then (at a fraction of the $15,395 plus HST Shipton’s had quoted for a new unit).

Strike Three (Bullying and Blackmail)

In December 2024 we had our annual furnace maintenance.

Before continuing, let me again point out that Shipton’s has maintained these systems for the past 20 years. During a maintenance visit in 2015 we were told that some furnace venting was no longer “up to code” but that we were “grandfathered in” and, legally, don’t have to keep up with evolving building codes (many homes are obviously in a similar situation). However, when this technician explained that some of the vent pipe material (PVC) is no longer used because it is prone to cracking and could create a hazard, we had them replace it with their suggested material (PBS) at our discretion.

Back to the December 2024 maintenance visit. At the end of this visit, on the invoice presented, the technician (Josh) wrote “immediate repair: 1) collector box ($470.25+HST), 2) venting repair ($1,399+HST), and 3) it may be time to have a home comfort adviser provide options on a more efficient system”. He also wrote “Furnace 1 has mixed venting that is against code”.

Let me remind you that 1) is the same component that a different technician (Pete) reported last year (“they tell us to write something”) and that when the technician (Pete) was dispatched last year with the collector box to do the repair, he told us “I don’t know why I am here, replacing the collector box won’t fix the leak in the unit”. Let me also remind you that, for a previous furnace venting issue noted by the same company in 2015, we were told that we were “grandfathered in”.

Then, out of the blue, the technician (Josh) hands my wife a “Safety Violation Notice” giving us 42 days to complete the repairs, which reads “If not corrected, the gas supply will be discontinued on DUE DATE and charges may apply”. And have the repairs completed by whom, you might ask?

I was out of the country and the technician (Josh) was appraised of the fact that I was called away to deal with a family emergency. Josh saw that we were in the midst of renovations and that the house was full of trades calling on my wife for various decisions, yet he insisted that my wife sign a document on the spot and would not leave until she did (with the specter of gas termination in the middle of winter hanging over her head). She said we would like time to think about this and get a second opinion since she had her hands full at the moment as he could plainly see, reminding him I had been called away on an emergency. She said that Shipton’s has been maintaining our systems on contract for the past 20 years and that it was her understanding that we were “grandfathered in”, and can this not simply wait until my return? He refused her request for either and would not leave until she signed a document.

In order to get Josh to leave our property (recall we are in the midst of renovations), my wife had to approach our contractor (who happened to be on site) and ask him to take time out of his day to go into the basement with Josh to review the issue. Only after that did Josh leave. Apparently, the technician was willing to reason with a middle-aged male contractor but not with an older retired female. This type of sexist and bullying behaviour is absolutely unacceptable. It is also a short-sighted business practice, presuming you rely on reputation, trust, and goodwill that is. Furthermore, the violation notice states “The appliance and/or piping does not constitute an immediate hazard”, so there was absolutely no reason to deny us time to discuss this issue upon my return before signing anything.

I sent the email below to Shipton’s early the following morning (6 AM) while abroad dealing with a family situation:

Greetings [],

After the third service visit in a row (specifically, scheduled maintenance last Fall, this Spring, and now this Fall) that were some of the most unpleasant experiences my wife or I have had with any company, ever, we are left with no choice but to terminate our longstanding relationship with your company.

Kindly cancel our service agreement, the automatic monthly withdrawals from our account, and also please send confirmation of so doing at year earliest convenience.

Thanks for your understanding, I look forward to receiving confirmation and we appreciate your timely attention to this unpleasant matter.

Crickets… zero response to date.

So, chalk this visit up to “intimidation, bullying, sexism and blackmail are our current business model”…

P.S., threatening to have our gas supply cut off in the middle of winter in order to gin up some business? Really Shipton’s, you have sunk to a new low with this one.

Epilogue

In retrospect, warning signs about dealing with this company had surfaced multiple times, and it is on us for not acting sooner. For example,

  • A few years ago a technician was dispatched by Shipton’s to perform routine maintenance. I asked how Shipton’s was treating them and they said they were going to work for another company. They added without any prompting that we have really good equipment and that Shipton’s advises their technicians to encourage clients to upgrade their equipment prematurely (which was one reason they were leaving as it did not sit well with them).

  • A former employee (not the one just mentioned) told us that Shipton’s use high pressure sales tactics and financially rewards technicians who successfully generate sales during their visits.

  • After the most recent event, a company recommended by our contractor examined the offending equipment, and guess one thing they noticed while inspecting the furnaces? That the furnaces were dirty and could use a good cleaning (they did not know we just had the systems cleaned). And when did this company examine the furnaces, you might ask? The afternoon of the same day on which Shipton’s had done their regular cleaning, maintenance, bullying and blackmail, i.e., just a few hours later!

Enough said.
